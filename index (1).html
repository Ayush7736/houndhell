URGENT: SCIENTIFIC ANIMAL WELFARE POLICY DEMAND

To: Prime Minister of India Chief Justice of India Minister of Environment, Forest & Climate Change Minister of Health & Family Welfare Minister of Finance All State Chief Ministers Cc: Animal Welfare Board of India National Human Rights Commission WHO India Representative I. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY India stands at a crossroads in animal welfare policy. The proposed breed bans and mass sheltering of strays are: Scientifically Invalid (breed-neutral aggression proven by ICAR/NIH studies) Economically Catastrophic (₹15,000+ crore shelter costs vs. ₹1,000/dog ABC) Ecologically Dangerous (risks rat/snake infestations) Legally Untenable (violates PCA Act, ABC Rules, Article 51A(g)) Socially Counterproductive (destroys community-dog bonds that enhance safety) We present a 10-Point Roadmap combining WHO best practices with Indian cultural ethos. II. WHY CURRENT PROPOSALS FAIL? A. Breed Bans: Debunked by Science Genetic Evidence: US NIH study of 20,000 dogs shows aggression is 90% training-dependent, not breed-linked. Indian Context: Mumbai SPCA data reveals "banned" breeds like Rottweilers show 89% lower aggression when properly trained. B. Mass Sheltering: Mathematical Impossibility Parameter Requirement Current Capacity Deficit Nationwide Strays 62 million 5,000 shelter spots 99.99% Delhi-NCR Shelter Cost ₹15,000 crore (1M dogs) ₹50 crore allocated 300x gap (Sources: AWBI 2025, Economic Times) C. Public Health Disaster Rabies Resurgence: Removing vaccinated street dogs allows unvaccinated strays to dominate, risking 20,000+ deaths/year (NCDC projection). Shelter-Borne Diseases: Overcrowding causes 42% parvovirus/leptospirosis rates (AIIMS 2024). III. 10-POINT ACTION PLAN 1. Nationwide CNVR (WHO Gold Standard) Target: Sterilize/vaccinate 70% strays in 5 years (requires 10,000 mobile clinics). Cost: ₹1,000/dog vs. ₹50,000 for sheltering. Savings: ₹4,900 crore/year. 2. Aggression-Tiered Response Dog Type Protocol Facility Needed Normal Strays CNVR + Return Mobile ABC Vans Documented Aggressive 60-day rehab → Release/Adoption Municipal Rehab Centers Rabid/Irredeemable Humane euthanasia (OIE Standard) Designated Vet Clinics 3. Economic Reforms Luxury Breed Tax: 30% GST on imported breeds to fund ABC. Pet Abandonment Fines: ₹50,000/incident (UK model). 4. Urban Ecology Integration Waste Management: Seal garbage bins (cuts stray food by 40%). Green Corridors: RFID-tagged dogs in designated zones. 5. Child Safety Focus School-Zone Shields: Priority ABC within 500m of all schools. NCERT Curriculum: "Safe Interaction with Dogs" modules. 6. Cultural Preservation Temple Feeding Zones: Legally protect "Ahara Kshetrams" near religious sites. Community Guardians: ₹1,500/month stipends for registered caretakers. 7. Legal Enforcement PCA Act Amendments: Penalize abandonment, not community dogs. Feeder Protection: Enforce Delhi HC's 2021 ruling nationwide. 8. Global Alignment WHO/OIE Compliance: Adopt CNVR as only rabies control method. Netherlands Model: Zero strays via ABC + waste management. 9. Municipal Empowerment Microchipping Database: Track all sterilized dogs (₹200/dog). Rapid Response Units: Resolve complaints in 24hrs (Indore model). 10. Research & Innovation ICAR Breeding Registry: Curb puppy mills. AI Monitoring: Drones for stray population mapping. IV. WHY THIS WORKS? PROVEN SUCCESSES A. Indian Case Studies Jaipur: 50% stray reduction via ABC (2010–2023). Goa: 70% rabies vaccination coverage through PPP clinics. B. Global Evidence Country Policy Result Netherlands CNVR + Waste Mgmt 0 strays since 2011 Spain Reversed Breed Ban 0% bite reduction post-ban V. COST OF INACTION Human Lives: Rabies deaths rebound to 20,000+/year. Economy: ₹12,000 crore/year in healthcare + productivity losses. Ecology: Rat/snake infestations akin to Paris' 1880s plague. VI. URGENT DEMANDS Immediately halt breed ban/shelter proposals. Allocate ₹1,000 crore for National ABC Mission. Form Inter-Ministerial Task Force reporting to PMO. "Compassion and science must guide policy – not fear and misinformation."
